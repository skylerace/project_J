Open
The question — back to screen, eyes on room
Stand at the front. Don't touch the screen yet. Make eye contact.
Say this
"Before I show you anything, I want to ask you one question. Think about your single best commercial title sales rep — the one who consistently brings in the most business. What do they do differently than everyone else on your team?"
Wait. Let them answer. You'll hear: "they know everybody" · "they're always ahead of the market" · "they hustle." Nod.
Then say
"Every answer you just gave me is an intelligence problem. Your best rep wins because they know more, sooner, than everyone else in the room. That intelligence is scattered across RCA, CoStar, Cotality, county records, and years of memory. Pulling it together takes hours. What I'm about to show you is what happens when every rep on your team has that same edge — in ten seconds."
Turn to the screen for the first time.
Act 1 · 2 min
Morning brief — you're on this screen
Say this
"This is what Jarvis shows your rep when they open their laptop at 8am. Not a generic dashboard. Intelligence synthesized overnight from three sources — RCA transaction history, CoStar active listings, and Cotality's CLIP database. 153 million parcels. Every commercial structure in the country. All tied together by a permanent parcel ID."
Point to the four metric cards across the top.
Say this
"$284 million in Q1 Lake County volume. 47 active listings. 23 parcels flagged with 7-plus year hold periods — Cotality telling us who is statistically overdue to sell. And a $148 million predicted Q2 pipeline. Before your rep makes a single call."
Point to the Priority Alerts card. Point to the first alert — the LLC dissolution.
Say this
"First alert this morning — an LLC dissolved on March 22nd. That's a Cotality CLIP ownership event. Jarvis saw a legal entity change on a 3.2-acre industrial parcel in Libertyville, cross-referenced the RCA ownership history, checked CoStar for an active listing — there isn't one yet — and flagged it as a score-94 deal signal. 94 out of 100 likelihood this property trades in 2026."
Pause two full seconds. Look at the room, not the screen.
Then say
"Your competitor's rep doesn't know about this yet. This rep does. At 8am."

Act 4 · 2 min
Live question — let them drive
Turn back to the room. Smile. This is the most powerful moment.
Say this
"I want to show you one more thing — and I'm going to let you drive it. Give me a real question. A property, a broker name, a market question — anything you'd actually want to know about Lake County commercial real estate right now."
Take their question. Type it into the chat input. Hit send.
While it loads
"This is a live AI with web search. It's not pulling from a fixed script — it's reasoning across the data in real time."
When it responds — read the key insight aloud. Then:
Then say
"That's the answer your rep gets in ten seconds instead of two hours."
If the room goes quiet after this, just move to the close. Don't fill silence with more demo.
Close · 90s
Step away from the screen
Face the room. Don't look at the screen. Slow down here.
Say this
"What you just saw is running right now, on a laptop, powered by the same commercial data your company already has access to through RCA, CoStar, and Cotality."
Then say — slow down here
"Your best rep already does a version of this manually. They spend two or three hours a day piecing this picture together. What would they do with those hours back? And what would your average rep be capable of — if they had the same intelligence your best rep has spent a decade building?"
One beat of silence.
Close with this
"That's Jarvis. Not a replacement for your reps. A force multiplier for all of them."
Stop. Don't fill the silence. Let them respond. If the room goes quiet, add this one line only if needed:

"How does it get our actual data?"›
In production, Jarvis connects directly to your RCA feed, your CoStar subscription, and Cotality's CLIP API. Everything you saw today uses real Lake County data structures — the production version replaces the demo records with your live feed.
"What does this cost?"›
That's the conversation I want to have with you — the build cost depends on what integrations you already have licensed. What I can tell you is the ROI math is straightforward: if this tool helps each rep identify two additional closings per quarter, the tool pays for itself in the first month.
"How long to build?"›
The prototype you saw today took days, not months. A production version with your live data connections is a 60 to 90 day build. The hardest part is already done — the intelligence layer, the scoring model, the UX.
"Is this secure? Our data is sensitive."›
Completely valid concern. The production architecture runs entirely within your own infrastructure — your API keys, your servers. Your data never leaves your environment. The AI processes queries but doesn't store or train on your proprietary data.
"We already have CoStar / RCA tools."›
Exactly — and that's the point. You're paying for those tools but your reps are spending hours manually pulling data across them. Jarvis doesn't replace those subscriptions. It makes them 10x more useful by connecting them together and surfacing the insight automatically.
"Can it work for other markets, not just Lake County?"›
Yes — Cotality CLIP covers 153 million parcels nationally. The Lake County build is the proof of concept. The same model scales to any market where you have RCA and CoStar coverage, which is most of the US.
"What if the AI gets something wrong?"›
Great question — and it's the right one to ask. Jarvis always cites which data source it's drawing from: RCA, CoStar, or Cotality CLIP. Your rep can verify any claim in seconds. It's a starting point, not a final answer — but it's a starting point that used to take three hours to reach.
